## User Journey Map

### Current State (Before Your Product)
[Describe step-by-step how users accomplish their goals today]

**Example:**
> 1. Team lead checks Jira board each morning
> 2. Manually reviews each ticket to see status
> 3. Sends Slack messages to check on blockers
> 4. Waits for responses (often hours)
> 5. Updates priorities based on responses
> 6. Posts updates in team channel
> 7. Repeats throughout the day
>
> **Time spent**: 2-3 hours/day
> **Pain points**: Reactive, manual, fragmented information

### Desired Future State (With Your Product)
[Describe how your product improves this journey]

**Example:**
> 1. Team lead opens dashboard with AI-generated daily summary
> 2. Reviews blockers, capacity, and risk areas (5 min)
> 3. Makes priority decisions based on recommendations
> 4. Team members get automatic updates on priority changes
> 5. Blockers automatically flagged and routed to right people
>
> **Time spent**: 30 min/day
> **Benefits**: Proactive, automated, centralized insights

## User Segments

### Segment 1: High-Growth Startups
- **Size**: 60% of target market
- **Characteristics**:
  - 20-200 employees
  - Fast-paced, limited process
  - Need to scale coordination quickly
  - Budget for tools if ROI is clear
- **Priority**: **High** (primary ICP)

### Segment 2: Established Tech Companies
- **Size**: 30% of target market
- **Characteristics**:
  - 200-2000 employees
  - Existing heavy tooling
  - Need better integration, not more tools
  - Longer sales cycles, higher ACV
- **Priority**: **Medium** (focus after PMF)

### Segment 3: Non-Tech Companies
- **Size**: 10% of target market
- **Characteristics**:
  - Engineering teams of 5-50
  - Lower technical sophistication
  - Budget constraints
  - Need simpler, more opinionated solutions
- **Priority**: **Low** (addressable later)
